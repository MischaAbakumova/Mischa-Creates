Liturgy

A multimedia dance performance developed at Lehman College as a part of “Dance in Dialog” series.

Ayo Okunseinde and I were invited to workshop with students new methods of exploring dance and body movement. At that time we were investigating the notion of technological sublime in front of machine intelligence. As a part of the challenge we offered students to speculate on the subject of religion, consciousness and technology. Opposing to the popular view of technology as evil, we imagined the scenario where it recognized humans as creators and worshiped them instead.

Chareorgraphy

Over 3 days, in collaboration with a choreographer Wendell Cooper, we developed a whole new vocabulary of body movement to represent the discussed ideas. We broken the narrative down in 5 sections: Genesis, Discovery, Growth, Ecstasy and Genesis. In our converations we came up with a couple of code words to describe each stage.

  • Genesis = Existence. Loop. Formation
  • Discovery = Mutation. Glitch
  • Growth = Acquisition. Knowledge. Ritual
  • Ecstasy = Self-awareness. Emotion

Throughout the performance, all dancers wore costumed made bracelets that were receiving signals from the main source, “totem”, as well as sending information about dancers positions in space, their acceleration rate, and pulse. We recorded data with the idea that we would use it to reconstruct performance from the spacial data points and use them as a blueprint for interpretation of the next iteration of the performance.
