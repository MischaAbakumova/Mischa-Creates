During my time at Blink Blink I created a series of projects and video tutorials centered around learning and understanding soft circuits through arts and crafts. The goal was to show kids that technology could be fun and exciting, and they don't have to write a single line of code to make awesome things. Each project involved simple electronics such as LEDs, fairy wires, velostat, copper tape, conductive fabric and ordinary household objects, encouraging children to be resourceful and think outside of the box.

At Blink Blink I taught new generation of makers at multiple workshops and masterclasses for diverse age groups of participants. Maker Faire and Chick Tech were just a few milestones.
As an innovative company led by women, Blink Blink puts a lot of effort into closing the gender gap in the male dominated fields of science and technology, providing girls with the safe space to explore their creativity.

If you want to learn more about how Blink Blink is changing the world of technology, visit blinkblink.cc
