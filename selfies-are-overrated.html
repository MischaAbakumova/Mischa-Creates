Sometimes I like to pick up my old film camera and go out into the world. I'd choose film over digital any day with its grainy texture and "unwanted" noise, because there is nothing that makes you think of the moment more than the precious roll of celluloid. Geometric patterns, reflections, religion, childhood are often the subjects caught in my lens. I have a particular fascination with people placed in geometrical rhythms created by the buildings outlines, mirrors and shadows.

Photography is also the essential part of my travels. New Yorker On The Run is where I keep the memories of most breathtaking places and moments from my escapades.
